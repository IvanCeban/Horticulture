What is the difference

It doesn’t matter the size of your garden or the environmental challenges it may present, there is a plant that will thrive in the conditions you have!
I am frequently told by my clients that “nothing will grow” or “we’ve tried several plants and all have died” when faced with ‘problem’ areas of the garden.

Other regularly used phrases I hear are “we already have a gardener but we’re unsure what to plant” or “we have recently had the garden landscaped but there are plants that are not doing very well”. This is the key difference between a landscaper, a gardener and a horticulturist. There is a misconception that these are the same. They are not.

I provide three horticultural services that will give you the garden that you have dreamed about.

Maintaining a garden is just as tough as it is getting it to the right level of beauty and harmony. We’ll take on that hard work so you don’t have to.

Our Mission is to make your gardens enjoyable again

I have worked and trained in the horticultural industry for the past 20 years covering all aspects of the sector. I have a genuine passion for plants and interest in gardens. I provide a service and experience targeted at the higher end of the sector, and it is this approach that sets me apart from other companies. I hold full Public Liability Insurance and the required licenses to safely undertake your domestic garden care.

I hold numerous industry-recognised qualifications with various trade organisations. These include City & Guilds in Amenity Horticulture, NPTC Safe Use and Competency in the Application of Pesticides, IOSH Managing Safety, LANTRA Chainsaw Maintenance and felling of small and medium trees, LANTRA Safe Use of Industrial Lift Plant Equipment. I am also a member of, and an approved company with, The Gardeners Guild.
