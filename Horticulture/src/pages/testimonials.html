“We were originally approached by our client, Fiona Mcleod, back in the early summer of 2017. Fiona is a freelance garden photographer based in Buckinghamshire who photographs gardens and plants for magazine publications and pre-commissioned projects.

A large proportion of her garden was hard landscaped and planted around 10 years ago using mainly herbaceous perennials and ornamental grasses. Since then, further hard landscaping had taken place, creating additional borders. The project was planned for autumn 2017 and involved cutting back, lifting, dividing and replanting the existing plant stock blended with new additions due to the original borders becoming congested.

A planting plan and scope of works was designed and issued to us by Fiona to complete the project.

We worked closely with the client to ensure the work was completed as per her drawings and within the desired timeframe and were on site for several days undertaking the project.”

“We were contacted by our client in the early spring of 2017. In the past the client had instructed several garden care providers to manage and maintain their extensive gardens with the most recent company being employed for approximately 8 years.

During this period, there had been several areas where the planting had been added to and amended. We were invited to discuss and review the planting with the client as they felt the garden lacked seasonal interest, colour, and plant variety. The brief was to ensure the garden reflected the seasons with interest throughout the year.

Our initial advice and recommendation to the client was to agree which plants to retain, those that were in need of rejuvenation, those that would benefit from relocation and those to remove.

We then designed, and worked systematically, through the garden creating opportunities to inject colour and seasonal interest that would reflect the time of year whilst creating focal points around the garden. This work included the preparation and enrichment of the soil, pruning, dividing, relocating and incorporating new plants.

On completion of the project, we were approached by the client to undertake their year round maintenance and attend fortnightly to manage the garden going forward. ”
